Who We Are

UL Computer Society provides students of the Univeristy of Limerick with a community of like-minded individuals who are passionate about technology, coding and gaming. We offer many services to our members including Skynet accounts, a Minecraft Server, use of the society's collection of equipment and more. The Computer Society arranges many events throughout the year. This year we have had events both in person and online. On this page you will find some of the highlighted events of the year.

Drone Night

On the 19th of September, 2019, we held a Drone Night where we invited three guest speakers, Oran Murray, a PhD researcher, and Ciaran O’Mara and Eoghan Mulcahy, founders of Deepsake.ai to talk about their work in idustrialised drone applications. Many of our members attended to hear the speakers talk about how they were using drones for new and innovative solutions. Kevin McAndrew, also talked about his winning Show & Tell project where he made a three-winged drone from scratch. Finally, we took a group picture with our DJI Mavic drones.

WordPress Tutorial

On the 2nd of October and 19th of February, we held WordPress tutorials for our members. Members were shown how to setup WordPress on their Skynet hosted websites. With WordPress installed, they could create a full website with little to no code. We also invited Haidee McMahon to one of the tutorials. She is the Director of Engineering at WP Engine Limerick. She gave a talk about WP Engine, the software we recommend and show people how to use to build websites on our server cluster Skynet.

#TeamTrees Fundraiser

On the 28th of November 2019, we organised a charity fundraiser for the #TeamTrees initiative. The goal of #TeamTrees was to plant 20 million trees around the globe by January 1st 2020. To raise funds for the project we set up our HTC Vive VR Headset in UL's Courtyard and held a Beat Saber tournament. We raised €100 from people on campus who visited our event, we matched that donation to €200. Which in the end allowed 217 trees to be planted all around the world.

Recruitment Drive

CompSoc sets up the network infrastructure for every club and society recruitment drive in UL. With over 5km of cable, 8 network switches and many wifi routers, setting up the recruitment drive is a hard yet rewarding task.

PC Build

On the 12th February 2020, after recieving all our custom PC components and parts, we held a PC Build event for our members. The PC cost €2000 in total. Our members were all given a chance to help out and we also setup our VR headset. We later held an event were we tested and attempted to overclock the PC's max CPU frequency.

Minecraft Build

On the 18th of March 2020, we organised an online social event where members of the society joined our Minecraft server to build UL's Foundation Building together. There was also a build competition with prizes for the best creation.

Finances

We had a massive boost in income and expenditure this year. We received money in sponsorship, from providing networking at the recruitment drive, membership, from our events and from budget allocation. Unfortunately, the second semester was cut short because of lock-down restrictions, massively reducing our income and expenditure. Our projected income is shown in red.
